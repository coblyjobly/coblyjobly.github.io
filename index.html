This platform will serve as a central resource for sustainability in Tallahassee, connecting users with local events, organizations, and businesses. An interactive map and calendar will showcase opportunities for community involvement, such as trash clean-ups, invasive plant removals, and other environmental initiatives to better the community.

Role: Project Facilitator Intern

Contributions: Leadership, Web Development, Databases, Python

Purpose

I am facilitating this project as part of my internship at the Florida State University Innovation Hub. The objective of this initiative is to equip students with foundational skills in web development and programming, providing them with hands-on experience in a field they may not have previously explored.

We chose to focus on creating an application that connects users with local environmental agencies to address the growing need for community engagement in sustainability efforts. By bridging gaps between Tallahassee residents and these organizations, we aim to enhance collaboration, promote awareness, and drive participation in local initiatives.

Skills Involved

  • Web Development: Proficient in building responsive web applications.
  • Technologies: HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • Version Control: Utilizing GitHub for collaborative development and version management.

Valley Of Unity

January - April 2024

Valley of Unity is an action-packed adventure game set in a mysterious land where no one can escape. You take on the role of a knight who uncovers a mystical feather, hinting at a way out. To break free, you must gather more feathers hidden throughout the Valley.

Navigate through a mix of top-down exploration in the overworld and intense side-scrolling combat in dungeons. Along the way, NPCs will guide you, pointing you towards the feathers and your ultimate goal—climbing the final mountain to unlock the truth behind the Valley. Made with Unity.

Contributions: Project Management, Game Design, C#, Unity, Sound Design, Sprite Art

Purpose

The Valley of Unity project was developed as part of my Software Engineering capstone course, aimed at creating an engaging and interactive gaming experience. The primary objective was to design a game that combines elements of adventure and combat, set in a unique world. Through this project, we aimed to showcase the importance of collaboration and creativity in software development, allowing each team member to contribute their strengths to create a cohesive and entertaining product.

Skills Involved

  • Game Development: Proficient in designing and implementing gameplay mechanics within Unity, utilizing C# for scripting.
  • Collaboration: Successfully worked in a team environment, leveraging the strengths of each member to enhance the project, including programming, design, and sound.
  • Problem-Solving: Addressed challenges in game design and programming, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable player experience.
  • UI/UX Design: Developed intuitive user interfaces to enhance player interaction and engagement within the game world.

Reflections

In Valley of Unity, I collaborated closely with a talented team consisting of Logan, Danny, Ryan, and Gustavo. My primary responsibilities included most of the programming and design, where I focused on creating an engaging gameplay experience. Logan and Danny contributed significantly to the programming and game design aspects, while Ryan took charge of designing the user interface menus, ensuring that they were intuitive and visually appealing. Gustavo enriched the game with his musical compositions, creating an immersive auditory environment.

We developed the game using Unity and C#, leveraging these powerful tools to create a blend of a top-down overworld and a 2D platformer with combat mechanics. Throughout the development process, I learned the importance of effectively delegating tasks based on team members' strengths, which greatly improved our workflow. Additionally, I gained hands-on experience collaborating on a shared repository using GitHub, which was essential for version control and tracking our progress.

If given the opportunity to continue this project, I would focus on refining the
combat system to provide a more fluid and responsive experience. Additionally,
I would expand the game world to create a richer exploration experience and
introduce challenging boss fights to elevate gameplay complexity.

Swap Dash

January 2021

In Swap Dash, you take on the role of a raccoon determined to retrieve your family's secret sandwich recipe from an evil sandwich factory. This fast-paced puzzle game immerses you in three levels, where you must navigate the factory's intricate layout. Equipped with a unique topping gun, you will shoot switches that alter the factory's environment, enabling you to manipulate the layout to your advantage. Strategy and quick thinking are essential as you solve puzzles to progress through each level and ultimately reclaim the family recipe. Made with GameMaker.

Awarded 1st Place at the Future Business Leaders of America District Competition

Contributions: Project Management, Game Design, Sprite Art, Sound Design, Game Maker Language (GML)

Purpose

Swap Dash was developed for an FBLA competition during high school, with the goal of creating a puzzle-based platformer that challenged players through switch puzzles and timers. The game was designed to encourage players to beat high scores, providing a competitive yet fun environment. This project allowed me to delve deeper into game development, applying my programming knowledge while collaborating with artists and musicians to deliver a polished product for the competition.

Skills Involved

  • Game Development: Implemented core mechanics and game logic using GameMaker.
  • Programming Languages: Proficient in GML (GameMaker Language) to build the game's features.
  • Team Collaboration: Integrated art and music, ensuring a cohesive final product through close teamwork.
  • Game Design: Designed levels featuring switch-based puzzles and timers to engage players with high-score mechanics.

Reflections

This project taught me the importance of creating clean, reusable code to efficiently build game levels and mechanics. I also learned how to collaborate effectively in a team where everyone had different strengths—while I focused on programming, my teammates contributed significantly to the game's art and music. If I were to continue this project, I would add more levels and unique mechanics to further challenge players and
enhance replayability.

Fatal Business: Legendary Adventure

January 2020

Fatal Business: Legendary Adventure is a beat 'em up game created for the Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA) to highlight their programs. Players take on the role of Mr. Business as he travels to different global conferences. Along the way, he battles evil business bots, representing various challenges in the business world. The game combines fun action with lessons on leadership and teamwork, making it an entertaining way to learn about the values of FBLA. Made with Unity.

Awarded 1st Place at the Future Business Leaders of America District Competition

Contributions: C#, Sprite Art, Background Art, Sound Design

Purpose

Fatal Business was developed for an FBLA competition during high school. The game focuses on fast-paced, action-packed gameplay with an emphasis on weapon upgrades that provide players with fresh experiences as they progress. Collaborating with a team, we aimed to create a dynamic and engaging game, with a strong focus on player progression and the excitement of leveling up through different weapons.

Skills Involved

  • Game Development: Developed core gameplay features using Unity.
  • Programming: Collaborated on scripting the weapon upgrades and mechanics in C#.
  • Sound Design: Created sound effects to enhance player immersion and the overall atmosphere of the game.
  • UI/UX Design: Contributed to user interface design to ensure smooth navigation through game menus.
  • Team Collaboration: Coordinated efforts with teammates working on music, art, and other design aspects.

Reflections

Working on Fatal Business taught me the value of integrating multiple game elements—such as weapon progression and immersive sound design—into a cohesive whole. I learned a lot about managing sound assets and working on game logic alongside a team. If we were to continue developing this game, I would focus on refining the level design, improving the fluidity of boss battles, and expanding the game's content to enhance both challenge and enjoyment.
